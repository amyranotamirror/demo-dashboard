What this tab is
This is your post-sale view — once a deal closes, the relationship moves here. The goal is keeping customers healthy, catching risk early, and growing revenue through renewals and expansion.
What NRR means
Net Revenue Retention (NRR) measures whether existing customers are growing or shrinking your revenue. 100% = zero net loss (churn cancelled out by expansion). Above 100% = customers are upgrading faster than they churn. Below 100% = you're losing ground even without losing customers outright.
How to read this tab
Start with the triage table — it shows which accounts need action today, ranked by health risk and MRR. Then check the promise tracker for product follow-throughs that are overdue. Revenue charts are for the manager's weekly review.
