Scatter Plot — Outlier Highlight

v1 — open in browser to compare variants

Problem: When a scatter plot has extreme outliers, the axis scale zooms out to fit them, making all normal cluster points tiny and nearly invisible. These variants explore different ways to visually call out outliers so both clusters and extremes are clearly readable.

Variant 1 — Color + Size + Shape

Needs is_outlier field

Outliers become large red diamonds; normal points are small blue circles. Simple and clean — most effective when outliers are few and clearly identifiable in the data model.

Variant 2 — Hollow Ring Annotation

Needs is_outlier field

All points look normal, but outliers get a bold red ring drawn around them. Visually striking without distorting the shape encoding — great for cluster analysis.

Variant 3 — Always-Visible Label

Needs is_outlier field

Outlier points have their group name always shown as a floating label, even without hovering. Ideal when you need to identify exactly which group is the outlier.

Variant 4 — Auto Z-Score Detection

No flag field needed

Automatically detects outliers using Z-score (|Z| > 2 on either axis) via a Vega-Lite transform. Combines ring + label. Adjust the threshold in the Holistics option panel.
